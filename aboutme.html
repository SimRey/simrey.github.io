About me

An introduction

Simone Reynoso Donzelli

I work at the European Central Bank in the Banknote Division, specifically in quality control and production. Before starting my professional career, I earned a master's degree in chemical engineering from the University of Waterloo in Canada, culminating in two first-authored publications. Alongside my formal education, I have developed strong programming skills through both self-guided learning and formal coursework, reinforcing my commitment to continuous learning and technical expertise.
Growing up in a bilingual environment, I effortlessly acquired fluency in both Spanish and Italian. Further enhancing my linguistic repertoire, I pursued formal education in English, German, and French during my schooling years. As a result, I am now fluent in nearly all of these languages, a skillset that not only enriches my global perspective but also underscores my adaptability and versatility in multicultural and multilingual settings.
